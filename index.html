🔑 Core Idea

Compare temperatures at the same location during 1900–1940 and 1960–2000

Difference = Temp₂ − Temp₁

If Difference > 0 → the location got warmer

🔗 What Is Paired Data?

🔗 Paired t

Same subject, two conditions

  • Before/after measurements on the same person
  • Different time periods at the same location
  • Same device in different environments
before/after
VS

📊 Two-sample t

Two independent groups

  • Two different groups of people
  • Two independent samples
  • Comparing different individuals
Group A vs Group B

💡 Today’s climate investigation uses a Paired Design — the same location across two time periods!

❓ Why Use a Paired Design?

❌ Without pairing...

Comparing Beijing in 1900 vs. Sydney in 1960

→ The difference might be caused by different locations!

✅ Paired design

Comparing Beijing in 1900 vs. Beijing in 1960

→ Geographic factors are controlled, so the difference comes from time!

🎯 Advantage of pairing: reduce confounding and improve precision

🤔 Analysis & Discussion

Q1: Look at the dotplot

On which side of 0 do most of the data points fall? What does that suggest?

Q2: If climate change did not exist...

What would we expect the mean difference (μ_d) to be?

H₀: μ_d = 0   (No change)
Hₐ: μ_d > 0   (the climate has warmed)

Q3: How far is our sample mean from 0?

Our sample mean is x̄ = _____，Is that distance large enough to reject H₀?

Q4: Limitations

Is our sample size large enough? What other factors might affect our conclusion?
